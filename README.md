# research
This is a folder of my research projects

1. Bluebike Development (report + presentation) 

Analyzed two blue bike stations’ usage patterns by applying a continuous time Markov Chain model – birth and death process with bluebike official trip data. We give suggestions on which station to fill to increase the overall revenue if bluebike had limited bikes to place during afternoon peak hours.

3. Sales prediction 

In this project, we tackled the challenging issue of developing a prediction model with sparse data, a common problem in the industry. Our goal was to predict sales per customer triggered by a specific promotion using a two-step modeling approach. First, we addressed the highly imbalanced dataset by applying logistic regression to predict the likelihood of a customer being influenced by the promotion. Once identified, we used linear regression to forecast the purchase amount for those customers. The model demonstrated exceptional performance, achieving a remarkably small mean error of less than -4. 

4. Master Thesis 

In addressing the complexities of large-scale time series prediction, which include high time complexity, noise, and incoherence across hierarchies, we developed a novel approach called Reconciliation with Neural Network (R-NN) for hierarchical forecasting. This method effectively mitigates these challenges by leveraging the power of neural networks to reconcile and align forecasts across different hierarchical levels. Our R-NN approach not only enhances the accuracy of predictions but also ensures coherence and consistency throughout the hierarchy.
